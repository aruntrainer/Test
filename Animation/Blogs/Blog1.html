Tony Stark (Robert Downey Jr) in the first Iron Man

Iron Man, the thorn in Terrence Howard’s back. Back in the day, Robert Downey Jr was on a long path of regaining his employability after his lifelong and very public struggles with drugs. The turning point arguably came with a supporting role in Disney’s Shaggy Dog (2005) alongside Tim Allen, as it marked the point where he had apparently redeemed himself enough to be employable by Disney, a company of conservative family values through and through. But leading roles remained scarce, even though things did continuously improve with a supporting role in Zodiac and the lead in hardly known movie called Fur alongside Nicole Kidman. When Marvel started looking for a lead in Iron Man, Robert Downey Jr was convinced he was the guy.

However, apart from Jon Favreau, he was also pretty much the only one convinced of this at the time. The newly set up Marvel studios had no wish to gamble with their first feature film, with the potential to become a franchise, and most certainly not by taking the risk of hiring an ex drug addict. Therefore, stars like Tom Cruise were discussed. When Jon Favreau mentioned RDJ to the production, Marvel’s response was “Under no circumstances are we prepared to hire him, for any prize.” Things arguably looked pretty grim for RDJ’s aspirations of becoming a middle aged comic book super hero.

So what is Terence Howard so upset about? He claims that while in a philanthropist mood when the project just took off and no lead was cast yet, he selflessly told Marvel to cut his pay by $1 million if they’d cast Downey in turn. However, after the success of the first film, Mavel wanted to reduce Howard’s role in the sequel and, consequently, paying him less than they did for the first one. Unacceptable, thought both Howard and his agent, but instead of renegotiating, Marvel simply cast Don Cheadle instead and fired Howard. In his desperation, Howard took to the phone and tried to contact Downey to convince him to stick his neck out for him as he had done for him before, but Downey never even picked it up.

Therefore, the story of Howard’s involvement with the Iron Man films and Marvel came to an abrupt end. Or so he says. What Howard seems to be most upset about now, though, is Downey’s ‘failure’ to respond to his calls and stand up for him and taking what was supposed to be Howard’s money, thus resulting in Howard missing out on at least $100 million! It is quite telling that Howard claims Downey took “his money” and that he seems to be sure that he would have made the same kind of money Downey did had he stayed cast. In fact, the only reason Downey was ever able to make that kind of money from the movies is probably simply down to an error of judgement by Marvel, who probably vastly underestimated how popular they would become within a few years time, thus allowing Downey a small percentage of the profits.
